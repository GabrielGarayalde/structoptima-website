Failure Modes

9 Checks are carried out for the beam-column member under compression and applied Moments. The score is a measure of the capacity of utilization of the load with respect to the capacity.

Failure Mode

Score Calculation

1(a). Member Compression Capacity Ncx

$$ = \frac{N^*}{\phi N_cx}$$

1(b). Member Compression Capacity Ncy

$$ = \frac{N^*}{\phi N_cy}$$

2. Member Moment Capacity Mbx

$$ = \frac{M_x^*}{\phi M_{bx}}$$

3. Section Moment Capacity Msy

$$ = \frac{M_y^*}{\phi M_{sy}}$$

4(a). Reduced Section Moment Capacity Mrx

$$ = \frac{M_x^*}{\phi M_{rx}}$$

4(b). Reduced Section Moment Capacity Mry

$$ = \frac{M_y^*}{\phi M_{ry}}$$

5(a). In-plane Member Moment Capacity Mix

$$ = \frac{M_x^*}{\phi M_{ix}}$$

5(b). In-plane Member Moment Capacity Miy

$$ = \frac{M_y^*}{\phi M_{iy}}$$

6. Member Biaxial Bending Capacity

$$ = [\frac{M_x^*}{\phi M_{cx}}]^{1.4} + [\frac{M_y^*}{\phi M_{iy}}]^{1.4} $$

Column Design to AS4100

The following program yields the optimal member size for a simply supported beam-column under combined axial compression and biaxial bending. The design checks are performed following the procedure outlined in AS 4100 (click on info for further details on the checks performed).

It is possible to fully laterally restrain the column by clicking "FLR". Additionally, the section types can be filtered by selecting the checkboxes under the "Steel Section" panel.

The top 3 sections are given in the "Results" panel at the bottom. These are given in order of the most highly stressed and thus the most effectively utilized. Expanding the toggle button gives additional information on the remaining failure modes in order of their "Score [%]".
